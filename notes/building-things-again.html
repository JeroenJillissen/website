Building things again

At some point in a design career you stop building things yourself. You get further from the code, hand off more, spend more time in reviews than in the work. That's fine — it's how teams scale. But something gets lost.

Claude Code changed that for me. Not because it writes perfect code, but because it lowers the cost of trying something enough that I actually try things. Last week I built a small prototype that would have taken a proper sprint to spec, design, and hand off. It took an afternoon. It's rough, but it's real — I can click it, share it, test it.

The thing I keep noticing is how much faster you learn when you can build the thing you're thinking about. Figma is good for a lot, but there's a gap between what you can make there and what you can test in a browser. That gap is shrinking.

I'm not replacing engineers. I'm getting back something I gave up a long time ago.
