Jeroen Jillissen – Design leadership at the intersection of Design and AI

I'm a principal designer at Relay.app, where I help shape how people create and automate with AI. Our goal is to make powerful agent-based workflows accessible to anyone, without requiring technical skills.

Before Relay.app, I was Director of UX for Android at Google, leading multiple teams across the platform — including Onboarding, Safety, Privacy, Gboard, and others. Working at this scale meant setting design direction across teams and products used by billions of people worldwide.

As UX Director of Google Workspace, I oversaw all communication and time management tools — Gmail, Chat, Calendar, Google+, Groups, and more. It's also where I first started designing with ML and AI, through features like Smart Compose, Smart Reply, and Gmail's tabbed inbox.

2021–2024

Android 12, 13 & 14 — For three consecutive Android releases, my team shaped the design language of the world's most widely used mobile platform. Android 12 introduced Material You — the most significant visual overhaul in Android's history, built around personalisation and dynamic colour — while 13 and 14 deepened that foundation with refined consistency across the ecosystem. Alongside the OS releases, we led UX direction across the Pixel 6, 6a, 7, 7a, and 8 lines, helping Pixel grow from a niche hardware experiment into a credible flagship. The work sits underneath everything: the system UI, the settings architecture, the interactions millions of people use every day without noticing them. That invisibility is the point.
